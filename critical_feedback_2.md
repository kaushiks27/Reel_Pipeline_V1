# Video Generation — Recurring Feedback Rules

> Generalized from review feedback on 23/03/2026. Apply these as standing checks for every future video generation.

## 1. Character Differentiation
**Characters in the same scene must be visually distinct.** Avoid generating faces that look alike for different characters (e.g., mother and child should have clearly different facial features, proportions, and age-appropriate appearance).

## 2. Audio Pacing & Lip-Sync Naturalness
**Speech in anchor videos must have natural, conversational pacing.** Avoid unnatural or unusually long pauses between words or phrases. If generating dialogue with lip-sync, verify that the audio timing sounds human and fluid, not robotic or halting.

## 3. Anchor Eye-Line / Gaze Direction
**The anchor must maintain direct eye contact with the camera at all times** (unless the script explicitly calls for looking elsewhere). Off-camera gaze breaks immersion and makes the anchor appear disengaged.

## 4. Emotional Consistency Between Characters
**Every character's facial expression must match the emotional context of the scene.** If a scene depicts a child complaining, the parent should not be smiling. If a scene depicts an upset child being comforted, the child should not appear happy. Review each generated frame for emotional coherence across all characters present.

## 5. Emotional Consistency Across Scene Types
**B-roll scenes that depict conflict, sadness, or frustration must avoid positive expressions (smiling, laughing) unless the narrative has shifted to resolution.** Always cross-reference the script's intended emotion before approving a generated clip.
