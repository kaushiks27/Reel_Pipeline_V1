### Step 8.3 — Transitions

**Source:** Lesson 54, Step 8 — "Apply transitions, pacing, and music"

```
TRANSITION GUIDE:
- Between most scenes: Quick cut (no transition) — keeps pacing fast for social media
- Between ANCHOR → B_ROLL: Quick dissolve (0.2-0.3 seconds) for smooth shift
- Between rules/sections: Subtle swipe or slide (0.2 seconds)
- NEVER use: Flashy/gimmicky transitions (star wipe, spinning, etc.)
- Keep transitions consistent — pick 1-2 types and stick with them

PACING RULES:
- First 3 seconds MUST be engaging (no slow fade-in)
- Social media pacing: fast enough to retain, warm enough to feel human
- Each scene should earn its time — if a clip feels slow, trim it in CapCut
- The end should feel resolved, not abrupt
```

### Step 8.4 — Add Captions/Subtitles

```
CAPTION INSTRUCTIONS:
- Use CapCut's auto-caption feature OR manually add captions
- Style:
  * Font: Clean, bold, easy to read (e.g., Montserrat Bold or similar)
  * Size: Large enough to read on mobile
  * Position: Center-bottom of screen (standard for Reels/TikTok)
  * Color: White text with black outline or drop shadow for readability
  * Background: Optional semi-transparent black bar behind text
- Sync captions to voiceover word-by-word or phrase-by-phrase
- Highlight the current word being spoken (if CapCut supports it)
- Verify captions match the script EXACTLY — no typos
```

### Step 8.5 — Add CapCut Effects

**Source:** Lesson 54, Step 8 — "Add CapCut Effects"

```
EFFECTS TO CONSIDER:
- Subtle zoom/scale keyframes on static clips (adds life to still moments)
- Light vignette on certain scenes for cinematic feel
- Color consistency filter across all clips (warm tone)
- Speed ramping: slightly slow down emotional moments, normal speed for action

EFFECTS TO AVOID:
- Heavy filters that change the photorealistic look
- Flashy text animations
- Overused TikTok effects that date the content
- Anything that makes the content look less professional
```

### Step 8.6 — Shorten / Lengthen Scenes

**Source:** Lesson 54, Step 8 — "Shorten / Lengthen scenes"

```
TIMING ADJUSTMENT GUIDE:
Watch the assembled video and check:
□ Does the hook grab attention in the first 2 seconds?
□ Is any scene too long (viewer would get bored)?
□ Is any scene too short (hard to process what's happening)?
□ Does the voiceover sync with the visuals scene by scene?
□ Is the CTA clear and not rushed?
□ Total duration within 30-50 seconds?

TO SHORTEN: Trim the end or beginning of a clip in CapCut
TO LENGTHEN: Add a slow-motion effect or freeze frame at the end
If a clip is way too short: consider combining with the next scene
```

## POST-PRODUCTION NOTES

### Engagement Strategy (from Lesson 87 — TikTok Engagement Hacks)

After posting, apply these engagement strategies:

1. **Filter comments**: Go to TikTok Settings → Comments → Filter keywords. Add phrases like "this is AI", "fake", "not real" — TikTok still counts filtered comments as engagement but hides them from public view.

2. **Seed early engagement**: Within 5-10 minutes of posting, use a second account to drop an opinion-based comment that creates a divide. Reply from your main account. This triggers the algorithm.

3. **60-minute engagement window**: Set a timer for 1 hour after posting. Reply to EVERY comment during this window. Turn 1 comment into 3-4 by asking follow-ups.

4. **Intentional micro-mistake** (optional): Include one small, subtle mistake that triggers people to "correct" you in comments. This dramatically boosts engagement.

- Prepare 2-3 versions with different hooks or CTAs
- Test which performs best
