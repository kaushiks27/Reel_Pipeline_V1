## INSTRUCTIONS FOR THE AGENT

### Step 7.1 — Generate the Full Voiceover (ElevenLabs)

**Source:** Lesson 49 — ElevenLabs & Sound Effects

```
VOICEOVER GENERATION SETTINGS:
- Tool: ElevenLabs
- Model: eleven_multilingual_v2
- Voice: [ELEVENLABS_VOICE_ID from Phase 0 setup]
- Voice Settings:
  * Stability: 65-70%
  * Clarity + Similarity: 75%
  * Style: 30%
  * Speed: Slightly slower than default (warm, measured pace)

INPUT TEXT: The complete script from Phase 2, including:
- All "..." pause markers
- Natural punctuation for rhythm (from Lesson 41)
- Example: "Rule one... greet people politely. This is called... manners."

QUALITY CHECKS:
□ Does the voice sound like a warm Filipino grandmother?
□ Is the pacing natural — not too fast, not robotic?
□ Do the pauses feel natural where the "..." markers are?
□ Is the total duration within the target (30-50 seconds)?
□ Does the pronunciation of Filipino-English phrases sound natural?
□ Is there enough variation in emphasis (not monotone)?
```

**Voice Quality Rules (from Lesson 41):**
- Standard AI delivery treats every word the same — this sounds robotic
- The "..." and punctuation in the script give the AI natural pauses
- The accent and tone settings limit the voice pool, which helps consistency
- If the voice sounds flat: add more punctuation, add "..." before emphasis words
- If still not right: Use ElevenLabs Voice Changer as fallback (Lesson 41)

### Step 7.2 — Match Voiceover to Scene Timing

```
TIMING SYNC CHECK:
For each scene from Phase 2, verify:
□ The voiceover words for that scene start and end within the scene's duration
□ There are natural breath pauses between scenes
□ The hook (first 3 seconds) is punchy and immediate
□ The CTA at the end doesn't feel rushed
□ Total voiceover duration matches total video duration from Phase 2

If timing is off:
- Adjust ElevenLabs speed setting (slightly faster or slower)
- Or adjust scene durations in the Phase 2 plan (update before proceeding)
- Regenerate voiceover if needed
```

### Step 7.3 — Prepare the Voiceover for Human Review

```
PRESENT TO HUMAN:
- The complete voiceover audio file
- A timing breakdown: which words play during which scene
- Duration comparison: voiceover length vs. planned video length
- Voice quality self-assessment

WAIT FOR APPROVAL:
- "APPROVED" → proceed to sound effects
- "REDO" + feedback → regenerate with adjusted settings
- "ADJUST SPEED" → regenerate at different speed
- "CHANGE VOICE" → try a different ElevenLabs voice
```

### Step 7.4 — Generate Sound Effects (ElevenLabs)

**Source:** Lesson 49 — "Generate new, unique sound effects on ElevenLabs"

```
SOUND EFFECTS TO GENERATE:
Review each scene and determine if it needs a sound effect.

Common SFX for parenting/family content:
- Soft "whoosh" for scene transitions
- Gentle chime or notification sound for each new rule
- Light background ambiance (home, outdoor, school)
- Subtle "ding" or emphasis sound for key moments

GENERATION:
- Tool: ElevenLabs Sound Effects generator
- Keep all SFX subtle — they support the content, never overpower it
- Match the warm, family-friendly vibe
- No dramatic/intense/scary sound effects
```

### Step 7.5 — Source Background Music (Pixabay)

**Source:** Lesson 54, Step 7 — "Add free music and sound effects from Pixabay"

```
BACKGROUND MUSIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Source: Pixabay (free, royalty-free)
- Style: Soft, warm, uplifting instrumental
- Mood: Family-friendly, nurturing, gentle positivity
- Duration: Must match the full video duration (30-50 seconds) or loop cleanly
- Volume: Low enough to sit behind the voiceover without competing
- NO lyrics (they compete with the voiceover)
- NO dramatic/intense tracks (doesn't match the warm vibe)

SEARCH TERMS TO USE ON PIXABAY:
- "gentle family"
- "warm inspirational"
- "soft background"
- "uplifting acoustic"
- "calm positive"

PRESENT TOP 2-3 OPTIONS to human with:
- Track name and link
- Duration
- Why it fits the vibe
```

### Step 7.6 — Compile Audio Asset List

```
AUDIO ASSETS INVENTORY:
1. Full voiceover file (.mp3 or .wav)
2. Individual sound effects (if generated)
3. Background music track (.mp3)
4. Timing breakdown document (which audio plays when)

All files should be clearly named:
- voiceover_full.mp3
- sfx_transition_whoosh.mp3
- sfx_rule_chime.mp3
- bgm_[track_name].mp3
```
