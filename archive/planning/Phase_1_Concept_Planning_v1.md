## WHAT THIS PHASE DOES

This phase defines what the video is about and who it is for. You will produce a complete concept brief before any script is written.

**Source (Lesson 54, Step 1 — exact coursework instructions):**
- Choose a niche or theme
- Define your audience
- Decide the format (short-form, long-form, cinematic, etc.)

- Have the finished video idea thought out roughly in your head

## INSTRUCTIONS FOR THE AGENT

### Step 1.1 — Choose the Topic

**Prompt to use (6-Layer Framework from Lesson 11):**

```
PURPOSE: I need a topic for a 30-50 second short-form vertical video (Facebook Reels / Instagram Reels / TikTok / YouTube Shorts).

ROLE: Act as a viral content strategist specializing in Filipino parenting and child psychology education content.

STRUCTURE: Give me exactly 5 topic options. For each, provide:
- Topic title (under 15 words)
- One-sentence hook (the first thing the viewer hears)
- Why this topic works for Filipino parents
- Emotional frame: what emotion drives the viewer to watch

STYLE: Warm, aspirational, action-oriented. Frame advice as "do this" — never "stop doing that."

DETAILS:
- Target audience: Filipino parents aged 25-45, especially mothers
- Every topic must produce ONLY positive, happy B-roll imagery
- Zero scenes of children in distress, sadness, fear, or isolation
- Hook must work through aspiration or promise (never guilt)
- Content must be culturally specific to Filipino family life

EXAMPLE of approved topic patterns:
- "X Habits That Build Confident Kids"
- "X Fun Ways to Bond With Your Child"
- "X Morning Routines That Set Your Child Up for a Great Day"
- "X Life Skills Every Child Should Learn by Age Y"
- "X Filipino Family Traditions That Build Strong Kids"
```

### Step 1.2 — NSFW Pre-Check on Chosen Topic

Before accepting the topic, run this mental filter (from Learnings Report, Section 2):

```
TOPIC SAFETY CHECKLIST:
□ Can EVERY B-roll scene show ONLY positive, happy imagery?
□ Does the topic frame advice as "do this" (not "stop doing that")?
□ Are there ZERO scenes requiring children in distress, fear, sadness?
□ Will the hook work through aspiration (not guilt)?
□ Is the content culturally specific enough to resonate (Filipino family context)?

If ANY checkbox fails → reject this topic and pick another from the list.
```

Present the checklist result to me for approval.

### Step 1.3 — Define the Audience

**Prompt to use:**

```
PURPOSE: Define the target audience profile for this video.

ROLE: Act as a social media audience strategist.

STRUCTURE: Provide:
- Age range
- Gender split
- Location / cultural context
- Pain points (what keeps them up at night)
- Desires (what they aspire to)
- Platform behavior (how they consume content)

STYLE: Specific and actionable. No generic marketing language.

DETAILS:
- This is for the Philippine market specifically
- Primary platform: Facebook Reels (largest Filipino audience)
- Secondary: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
```

### Step 1.4 — Decide the Format

This is locked for our pipeline:

```
FORMAT DECISION (FIXED):
- Type: Short-form vertical video
- Duration: 30-50 seconds
- Aspect ratio: 9:16
- Resolution: 1080×1920
- Structure: HOOK (first 3 seconds) → BODY (rules/habits/tips) → CTA (last 3 seconds)
- Pacing: Platform-native for TikTok/Reels — fast enough to retain, warm enough to feel human
```

### Step 1.5 — Write the Basic Story Outline

**Prompt to use:**

```
PURPOSE: Write a basic story outline for this video.

ROLE: Act as a short-form video director planning a 30-50 second reel.

STRUCTURE: Provide:
- HOOK (0-3 seconds): What the viewer sees and hears first. Must stop the scroll.
- BODY (3-45 seconds): The main content. Break into numbered rules/habits/tips.
- CTA (last 3-5 seconds): What the viewer should do after watching.
- Estimated scene count (aim for 8-15 scenes based on the reference video analysis)
- Rough timing per section

STYLE: Think like a filmmaker. Describe what the viewer SEES, not just what they HEAR.

DETAILS:
- The anchor character (Lola) appears in 30-40% of scenes (talking to camera)
- The remaining 60-70% are B-roll scenes illustrating each rule/habit
- Every B-roll scene must depict positive, happy, family-friendly moments
- Reference video structure: ~51 scenes in 50 seconds, ~1 second average per scene
```
