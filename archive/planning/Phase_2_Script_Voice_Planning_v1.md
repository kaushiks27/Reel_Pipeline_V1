## WHAT THIS PHASE DOES

Write the actual video script and decide on narration/dialogue. Break the story into scenes with timing, camera angles, and emotional direction.

**Source (Lesson 54, Step 2 — exact coursework instructions):**
- Break down the story into scenes or clips (duration, POV, action)
- Write voiceover or character lines
- Format timing (e.g., 6 × 5-second clips = 30s video)
- Ask ChatGPT to prepare camera angles, lighting, and scenes
- Brainstorm about the vibe you want the script to give off (dark, funny, romantic etc)
- Ask ChatGPT to refine any areas that are lacking

## INSTRUCTIONS FOR THE AGENT

### Step 2.1 — Write the Full Script

**Prompt to use (6-Layer Framework from Lesson 11):**

```
PURPOSE: Write a complete voiceover script for a [DURATION]-second short-form vertical video about "[TOPIC FROM PHASE 1]".

ROLE: Act as a world-class short-form video scriptwriter who specializes in educational parenting content for Filipino audiences. You write scripts that are warm, clear, and immediately actionable.

STRUCTURE: The script must follow this exact structure:
- HOOK (first 3-4 seconds): One powerful opening line that stops the scroll. Use aspiration or a bold promise.
- BODY: [NUMBER] rules/habits/tips, each with:
  - A short rule statement (under 10 words)
  - A one-line explanation of why it matters (under 15 words)
- CTA (last 3-4 seconds): A clear call to action — "Share this with another parent" or "Follow for more tips."

STYLE: Warm but authoritative. Like a wise grandmother giving advice over dinner. Simple words — no jargon. Written to be SPOKEN aloud, not read.

DETAILS:
- Total word count: 90-120 words (for a 30-50 second voiceover at natural pace)
- Language: English with Filipino-English natural phrasing
- Add natural pauses using "..." between key phrases (from Lesson 41 — this gives the voiceover rhythm)
- Each rule must be actionable ("teach your child to..." not "children should...")
- No negative framing — every rule describes a POSITIVE behavior to build

EXAMPLE of natural rhythm with pauses:
"Rule one... greet people politely. This is called... manners."
"Rule two... always return items to where they belong... after using them. This builds... habits."
```

### Step 2.2 — Break Script Into Scenes

**Prompt to use:**

```
PURPOSE: Break this script into individual scenes with timing, visual direction, and scene type.

ROLE: Act as a short-form video director planning every shot.

STRUCTURE: For each scene, provide:
- Scene number
- Scene type: ANCHOR (Lola talking to camera) or B_ROLL (illustrative visual)
- Duration in seconds
- Voiceover text for this scene (exact words from the script)
- Visual description: What the viewer SEES during these words
- Camera angle suggestion (from the Kling Camera Movement Toolkit)
- Emotional tone of the visual

STYLE: Think cinematically. Every scene should have visual PURPOSE — it illustrates or reinforces the words being spoken.

DETAILS:
- Target: 10-15 scenes total
- ANCHOR scenes: 30-40% of total (Lola talking to camera)
- B_ROLL scenes: 60-70% of total (illustrating each rule/habit)
- Each scene: 2-5 seconds
- First scene (HOOK) must be ANCHOR — Lola looking directly at camera
- Last scene (CTA) must be ANCHOR — Lola with warm closing
- Camera angles to choose from (Lesson 33 — Kling Camera Toolkit):
  * Slow dolly in (emotional intensity)
  * Medium tracking shot (following action)
  * Close-up push-in (realization/emphasis)
  * Static wide shot with foreground movement (establishing)
  * Over-the-shoulder (perspective/dialogue)
  * Low angle (power/confidence)
  * Crane shot up (reveal/transcendence)
- Every B-roll visual must show ONLY positive, happy imagery
- Append NSFW safety clause to every visual description
```

### Step 2.3 — Define the Vibe

**From Lesson 54, Step 2:** "Brainstorm about the vibe you want the script to give off."

```
VIBE DEFINITION:
- Overall mood: Warm, nurturing, empowering
- Color palette: Warm tones — golden hour, soft daylight, earth tones
- Lighting style: Natural daylight, soft window light, warm lamp light
- Setting style: Filipino home environments, outdoor family spaces, school settings
- Character energy: Calm confidence, maternal warmth, gentle authority
- Music mood: Soft, uplifting, family-friendly instrumental
```

### Step 2.4 — Refine the Script

**From Lesson 54, Step 2:** "Ask ChatGPT to refine any areas that are lacking."

After the scene breakdown is complete, run this refinement check:

```
PURPOSE: Review and refine this script and scene plan.

ROLE: Act as a ruthless short-form content editor.

STRUCTURE: Check each of these and fix any issues:
1. Is the hook strong enough to stop scrolling in the first 2 seconds?
2. Does every rule/habit feel immediately actionable?
3. Are there any words that would sound unnatural when spoken aloud by a Filipino grandmother?
4. Is the pacing right — not too rushed, not too slow?
5. Does the CTA feel natural and warm (not salesy)?
6. Are there any B-roll scenes that could trigger NSFW flags?
7. Is the total word count between 90-120 words?

DETAILS:
- If any rule sounds preachy, make it warmer
- If any B-roll scene describes negative imagery, rewrite it as positive
- If pacing feels off, adjust scene durations
```
