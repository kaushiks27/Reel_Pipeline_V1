# Experience — Phase 1 Concept & Planning

## Reverse Prompting Q&A (March 19, 2026)

Q1: Is this the first video or part of a series?
A: First video ever.
Rationale: Topic should work as a channel intro — universally appealing, establishes Lola's voice and the channel identity.

Q2: Specific topic or generate options?
A: Generate options, BUT with critical visual style direction from client brief:
- **Animated** style, NOT photorealistic
- "Soft Pixar-adjacent illustrations" with expressive Filipino characters
- Bright, warm-lit, approachable — not cinematic, not dark
- Recognizable Filipino settings: modest homes, school classrooms, barangay courts, family dining tables
- Captions with highlighted keywords on screen throughout
- Listicle format with anchor character intercut with illustrated B-roll
- Must feel like something a Filipino parent would stop, save, and share on Facebook
Rationale: THIS CHANGES ALL IMAGE PROMPTS. The Lesson 72 realism triggers (photorealistic, visible pores, etc.) are NOT the right style. Nanobanana PRO prompts must shift to animated/illustrated style instead.

Q3: How many tips?
A: 3 tips per video.
Rationale: More breathing room per tip. ~10 seconds per tip + hook + CTA fits 30-50 seconds comfortably.

Q4: Language?
A: English.
Rationale: Matches ElevenLabs voice configuration and Veo blueprint.

Q5: CTA style?
A: Follow-for-more (default).
Rationale: Growth-phase content — simple, low-friction.

## Key Learnings to Carry Forward
- **CRITICAL: Visual style is ANIMATED, not photorealistic**
- Lola reference image (photorealistic) may need a companion animated version
- All B-roll prompts use animated illustration style, not the Lesson 72 photorealism triggers
- Filipino cultural specificity in settings, clothing, food, family dynamics
