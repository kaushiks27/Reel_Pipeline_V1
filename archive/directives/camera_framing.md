# Camera & Framing Best Practices — Directive

> Grounded in Lessons 22 (UGC framing), 33 (30 camera techniques), and 36 (Kling 4-field structure)

## Anchor Character Framing (from Lesson 22 patterns)

**Best practice extracted from 20+ UGC prompts in Lesson 22:**

| Framing | Usage | Source Examples |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| **Medium close-up (face + shoulders)** | Default for talking-to-camera anchor | "chest-up framing, eye-level angle" (lifestyle influencer prompt) |
| **Waist-up** | When showing hand gestures or product | "framed from the waist up, facing forward" (Instagram portrait prompt) |
| **Extreme close-up (face fills frame)** | Emotional emphasis, CTA moments | "extreme close-up, filling the frame" (Y2K influencer prompt) |

**For Lola (our anchor):** Default to **medium close-up (face + shoulders)**, direct eye contact with camera. Use waist-up when she gestures. Use close-up for the CTA moment.

## Kling 4-Field Prompt Structure (from Lesson 36)

**Every video generation prompt MUST use this exact 4-field format:**

```
Shot: [camera angle + framing]
Details: [what's happening, subjects, environment details]
Camera: [specific camera movement from Lesson 33]
Mood: [emotional tone]
```

**Example (from Lesson 36):**
```
Shot: Medium close-up from the side
Details: Subtle nod from the driver, fabric textures, carbon fiber, small dust particles floating
Camera: Slow push-in
Mood: Professional, serious, locked-in
```

**Important (from Lesson 36 tip):**
> "Always start your prompt with the camera angle and build from there. Always create your images in a way that will complement the camera movement you have in mind."

This means: when writing image prompts (Phase 3), design the composition to match the camera movement planned for that scene.
