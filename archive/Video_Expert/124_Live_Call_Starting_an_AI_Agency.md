COOPERATION AGREEMENT
FOR THE DEVELOPMENT OF AN AI-BASED WEB APPLICATION
Article 1
With this Cooperation Agreement (hereinafter referred to as: the Agreement) the contracting parties regulate their mutual rights and obligations as regards the provision of design and development services, which the Agency hereby undertakes to perform for and on behalf of the Client.
Article 2
In accordance with the activities defined in Article 1 of this Agreement, the Agency hereby undertakes to perform the following services:

2.1. Agency services

2.1.1 Design

2.1.1.1. UI Design of the web application
The Agency will design the user interface (UI) of the web AI-based application from scratch, delivering clear, visually detailed designs that provide the development team with comprehensive guidance for implementation.

2.1.2. Development

2.1.2.1. Backend Development
The development of the backend system will be done with Node Typescript as the programming language and NestJS as the chosen framework. The selection is driven by the desire to have a single language between the frontend and backend to simplify development as well as leveraging end to end typing from the database all the way through the view layer as provided by Typescript. Moreover, the PostgreSQL database will be employed to store and manage the application’s data, as it is renowned for its reliability, scalability, and adherence to industry standards.

2.1.2.2. Frontend Development
The frontend development will be undertaken utilising the React framework with server side rendered React Router. The choice of React stems from its component-based architecture and efficiency, enabling the creation of a scalable, maintainable, and high-performance web application. Using server side rendering through React Router will ensure the app loads faster than client side loading. This approach will ensure consistency in the application’s user interface and user experience, while streamlining the development process.

2.1.4. Project Management & Quality

2.1.4.1. Project Coordination
The Agency will oversee and coordinate all project activities to ensure tasks are delivered on time, within scope, and aligned with the Client’s requirements.

2.1.4.2. Quality Analysis
The Agency will conduct continuous reviews and testing to ensure the application meets agreed standards, is reliable, and performs as expected.

NOTES:
1. This Agreement covers scope changes that account for 5% of the project’s scope. Changes allowed are limited to visual aspects, like adjusting the screen layout, and cannot include functional modifications, such as adding different authentication methods.
2. All activities listed in Article 2.1 refer to pure Agency work and do not include any type of cost of third parties such as travel cost and accommodation, venues, transport, catering, etc.
3. All cooperation exceeding the regular cooperation as defined in 2.1. will be considered as separate projects and as such will be subject to the terms defined in 2.2. entitled Other activities

2.2. Other activities
As regards all other activities that exceed those defined in 2.1., the cooperation would be based on a cost estimate, the third parties’ costs, pre-approved by the Client, and as well as standard agency commission on the third parties’ costs. Any additional scope of work may be charged by the hourly rate of the Agency’s employees. The Agency’s hourly rate per employee is 80 USD (eighty United States Dollars).

2.3. Project Deliverables
As the outcome of the Agency Services and as the basis upon which this Agreement will come to terms, the Agency is obligated to provide the deliverables defined in the project specification.
The detailed specification of the AI web application is set forth in Exhibit A (Project Specification), which shall form an integral part of this Agreement.

Article 10 – Non-Poaching
During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following its termination or expiration, neither party shall, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, hire, or attempt to hire any employee, contractor, or key personnel of the other party who was involved in the performance of this Agreement, without the prior written consent of the other party.
This restriction applies to any individual who performed services under or in connection with this Agreement at any time during the twelve (12) months preceding the solicitation or hiring attempt.
In the event of a breach of this Article, the non-breaching party shall be entitled to liquidated damages equal to twelve (12) months of the solicited individual’s gross compensation, in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity. The parties acknowledge that this amount represents a reasonable estimate of the damages that would be incurred as a result of such breach.
This restriction shall not apply to individuals who respond to general public advertisements or job postings not specifically targeted at the other party’s personnel.
Article 11
Contracting parties conclude this Agreement for a period of 6 months, beginning on the 12th of February, 2026, and ending on the 12th of August, 2026, with a possibility to extend the Agreement.
Any amendments to this Agreement are valid only if composed in written form and signed by the authorised representatives of the contracting parties.
Article 12
Contracting parties will try to resolve all possible disputes resulting from the content of this Agreement or in relation to it by coming to a mutual agreement; otherwise, they will agree to the employment of the Croatian legislation and the jurisdiction of the actually competent Rijeka-based court.
Article 13
This Agreement is composed of one identical digital copy, which will be kept by each of the contracting parties, and will come into force on the 12th of February, 2026.
