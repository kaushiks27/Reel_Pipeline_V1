Engagement Cheat Code 2:
Here’s one of the most underrated growth hacks I’ve personally used to go viral
multiple times on TikTok — and the crazy part? It costs nothing.
Step 1: Post your video and let it sit for 5–10 minutes
 Step 2: Grab a second phone (or use a different device or account not linked to
your main one)
 Step 3: Head to your video and drop a comment — but don’t make it random. Ask a
question that creates a divide. Something opinion-based. Something that triggers
people to take a side.
 Step 4: Switch back to your main account and reply to that comment — start
debating. Literally argue with yourself if you have to. Stir the pot.
 Step 5: Once people start jumping in, keep it going. Reply to them from both
accounts. Fuel the conversation. Fan the flames. You’ll rack up 5–10+ comments in
minutes.
(Make sure you aren’t connected to the same WiFi, use 4G if needed)
This works like a charm. I’ve used it repeatedly — and I’m telling you, it never fails.
The psychology behind it is simple: most people don’t want to be the first to
comment. They’ll scroll past unless they see others already talking. People are way
more likely to engage when they feel like they’re joining an existing discussion
instead of starting one from scratch.
By planting those early comments and sparking a mini-debate, you’re hacking the
algorithm’s engagement signals. TikTok sees the activity — replies, likes,
interactions — and flags your video as active. That signal can be the difference
between a video that caps at 300 views and one that shoots past 10k+.
And the key is timing. TikTok gives you a short window (usually the first 1–2 hours)
where early engagement matters a lot. That’s your golden opportunity to get noticed
— and this tactic gives you a way to manufacture momentum before the crowd
arrives.
Don’t underestimate this. Start doing it consistently and watch what happens to
your reach.

Engagement Cheat Code 3:
Following on from the last cheatcode...
Most people upload their video… maybe reply to one or two comments… and then
bounce. Big mistake.
What you should do instead: Treat every single comment like it’s the start of a
conversation — especially in the first 60 minutes.
Once you’ve posted your video and go the comments rolling in from your own
commenting, set a 1-hour timer. During that window, your only job is to engage.
Hard.
 The second someone comments — anything, even if it’s “🔥” or “lol” — reply
instantly.
  Don’t just say “thanks.” Turn it into a thread. Ask a follow-up. React with a twist.
Make a joke. Say something unexpected.
 Repeat this with every comment. Your goal? Turn 1 comment into 3 or 4. Create tiny
little micro-conversations all over your video.
  If someone’s being negative? Even better. Keep it respectful, but go back and forth
with them. Other people will jump in. You're feeding the beast.
Think about it — when TikTok sees 20 people comment, but the comment count
says 70, it’s because you worked the section like a pro. The algorithm doesn’t know
who’s who — it just sees engagement spiking early and starts pushing your content
out wider.

Engagement Cheat Code 4:
I’ve mentioned it earlier on in the course, but here’s one of the sneakiest TikTok
growth tricks that’s worked for me more times than I can count:
Make a small, subtle mistake on purpose, or include something that is so out of
place that people cant help but correcting it.
Yeah, seriously.
Step 1: Post a video like normal — could be AI, educational, storytelling, opinion-
based, anything
 Step 2: Somewhere in the video (best option), text, speech, or captions, insert a
“mistake” — just enough to trigger the viewer’s urge to correct you
 Step 3: Sit back and let the comments roll in as people try to “educate” you, little
do they know that you are one step ahead
This can be as simple as:
Putting a Nike logo on a shoe, in a video thats meant to be in 1950
Saying a quote wrong (“Einstein invented gravity”)
Messing up a date (“The internet was created in 1995”)
Misnaming something (“This is Saturn” when it’s clearly Jupiter)
Adding a random giraffe for a split second in the background
Misspelling a basic word in a caption (“Your” instead of “you’re”)
Now, here’s why this works like crack for engagement:
Most people scroll past videos without commenting. But the second they spot
something “wrong,” their ego kicks in. They have to say something. It’s a reflex.
They feel smart for noticing, so they drop a comment calling it out.
And when one person does it, others join in.
You just created a debate zone without needing to ask for anything. Every
“correction” counts as a comment — even the snarky or rude ones. And TikTok
doesn’t care if the engagement is negative or positive. It just sees activity and
pushes your video harder.
