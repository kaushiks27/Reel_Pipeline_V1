Further Tips

To add to what Brody has mentioned, here's my top tips when cloning yourself:

Don't over-do it with the hand movements! You want some in there, but if you do a really strong movement, or repeat a movement too often, the AI will train to it and do it way too often in your final videos. The final result won't be natural

If one part of your video generation looks bad, you usually don't have to re-generate the whole thing. Just add some B-roll over the top, keep the clone talking but cut to the B-roll. This is how you should be editing your videos anyway for maximum retention. Cloned videos work best when mixed with B-roll, you shouldn't just have a solid video of your clone talking for 1 minute straight - it will look unnatural.

Watch the explainer video on the Heygen Clone page before generating, and follow all of the instructions

If it wouldn’t sound normal said out loud, it won’t lip-sync well. Keep your language natural, remember that it's meant to be realistic and sound like it's you talking 

If you feel that your clone is speaking too slowly or unnaturally. You can speed up the clone / audio in Capcut (or your favourite editing software) when you are editing the videos. Usually setting it to around 1.1-1.2 speed will solve this issue.
