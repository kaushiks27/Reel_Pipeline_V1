Consistent Character Voices in Veo 3.1

0:13
The trick with keeping voices consistent in Veo 3.1 is basically treating the voice like a locked character asset, not something you describe fresh every time.

Where most people mess up is they'll tweak the voice description between prompts. Even tiny changes, like swapping a word or two, can make the model give you a completely different tone or pitch. 

It's annoying but that's how it works. What you want to do instead is define the voice once with really specific traits. 

Examples: Accent, tone, pacing, personality. 


Something like: "light Australian accent, relaxed and confident, medium pace, bit of vocal rasp." 



Then copy paste that exact description into every single prompt. Basically think of it as a voice blueprint. Define it once and just reuse it across every scene (for that same character)

Also, keep your dialogue lines short and natural. This helps the avatar stay in rhythm. If you upload too much dialogue in each prompt, the avatar only has 8 seconds to say all of those words, so they are going to speak super fast, which doesn't sound natural at all.

Why default AI voices sound bad
Standard AI delivery treats every single word the same, there's no emphasis on the words that matter, no natural pauses where a real person would breathe, no melody in how sentences rise and fall, your brain picks up on this immediately. 

In the prompt above I add "..." and punctuation quite often. This let's the AI know that it needs to pause naturally between speech. The more punctuation the better, it gives the prompt rhythm.

If you don't add any sort of punctuation it will just sound like they are reading from a script, which is bad.

Then we finish the prompt with the "light Australian accent, relaxed and confident, medium pace, bit of vocal rasp." which locks in the tone and sound of the voice.



Why are accents important?

When you add an accent, you're changing the entire delivery system. These are tiny micro details that your ear processes subconsciously. Even a subtle accent is enough to transform a flat robotic voice into something that actually sounds like a real person talking. 

Think about it, there is probably only around 2-3 different female voices in Veo 3.1 for an australian accent (or any accent), so it's quite limited. 

This is a good thing, because it means that if you choose your accent, then the software doesn't have much choice but to continually give you the same sort of voice.

REMEMBER - YOU NEED TO CHOOSE AN ACCENT THAT MATCHES YOUR CHARACTER.
