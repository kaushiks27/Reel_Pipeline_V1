I would also like to sincerely thank @Daniel Riley, @Matt Stark, and @Graham Riley for their support and for building such an amazing community — one that not only teaches but also provides feedback and answers every kind of question to help people grow.

### Key Points (Personal Experience):

1. **Choose a niche you genuinely care about.** Your content should align with your interests and should not conflict with your profession or values.
2. **Today, almost 70% of the technical work can be supported by AI.** However, it still requires your creativity, daily effort, consistency, and strong time management.
3. **Many people struggle to find a new niche.** In today's AI-driven era, discovering niche ideas is easier than ever. You can generate dozens — even hundreds — of niche ideas using AI tools. Additionally, while scrolling on social media, stay observant. If you discover a niche that interests you, explore it and present it in a unique way.
4. **When it comes to short-form videos, the hook is the most critical element.** If your hook is weak, there is a high chance your reel will fail. Ideally, your hook should be 2–3 seconds long. In my experience, anything beyond 5 seconds can significantly reduce performance — even if the rest of the video is strong.
5. **Your hook should make viewers stop.** It can be emotionally appealing, surprising, bold, or thought-provoking — but it should never feel ordinary or generic.
6. **In short-form content, each clip should naturally lead to the next.** Your video must be engaging, not just informational.
7. **Video length matters significantly.** For viral potential, 15–30 seconds is often ideal. Watch time and average retention send strong signals to the algorithm about whether your video should be pushed further.
8. **Views alone do not make a video viral.** Your content must provide value so people feel encouraged to save, share, and comment. These actions send powerful signals to the algorithm.
9. **Focus on continuous improvement.** In the beginning, everyone makes mistakes. Analyze your best-performing reels — understand what worked and recreate similar content in a fresh way, since your audience has already shown interest in that format.
10. **Build a connection with your audience.** Reply to comments — especially in the early stages. In my opinion, even up to 100K followers, creators should try to respond as much as possible. This builds loyalty and encourages viewers to return.
11. **Many valuable content ideas come directly from comments.** Never ignore them. Comments help you understand whether your content resonated with your audience.
12. **There are four major platforms: YouTube, Facebook, TikTok, and Instagram.** Distribute your content across all of them instead of relying on just one. Once any platform's algorithm starts pushing your content to the right audience, growth can accelerate quickly.
13. **In today's AI era, video quality matters more than ever.** Every scene should be engaging — not just the hook — to prevent viewers from skipping.
14. **Your content should provide value.** Even if it is entertainment-focused, it must offer something engaging, relatable, or meaningful.
15. **Many creators make the mistake of deleting underperforming videos.** This is not a good practice. Avoid deleting content simply because it did not perform well initially.
16. **Do not repeatedly watch your own videos or constantly check analytics.** Instead, focus on improving your content. Also, make sure your page settings are optimized — including your bio, professional mode, category, and about section — as these help signal your target audience to the algorithm.
