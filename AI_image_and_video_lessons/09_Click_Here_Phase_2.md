## The Two Main Image Engines

In this phase, we focus on two major platforms:

- **Midjourney**
- **Nanobanana Pro**

They do different things well.

### First — Do I Need Both Tools?

**No.**

You do **NOT** need both.

You can test them, understand the difference, and then choose **ONE** (or mix them if you want).

The goal of this phase is for you to try them, feel the difference, and choose what fits your style.

---

## 🖌 Midjourney

Midjourney is where you create **art, mood, atmosphere, characters, worlds, and cinematic visuals**.

Midjourney is more artistic than Nanobanana. Midjourney is perfect if you love:

- Cinematic scenes
- Stylized or artistic visuals
- Characters, worlds, fantasy, realism, sci-fi
- Mood, lighting, atmosphere

> If your goal is to make things that look **beautiful, dramatic, or story-driven**, Midjourney will probably feel natural to you.

**Think of Midjourney as your artistic partner.**

---

## 🧪 Nanobanana Pro

Nanobanana is where you create **control, consistency, and commercial-grade visuals**.

This is the tool you use when you need:

- Product shots
- Text that stays perfect
- Logos that don't melt
- Consistent characters
- Brand-consistent visuals
- Ads that actually look professional

> If your goal is to make things that are **usable for brands, products, and marketing**, Nanobanana will probably feel better.

**Think of Nanobanana as your production studio** — it gives you reliability and accuracy.

However, it can also do artistic stuff, so it's a **good all rounder & the tool I recommend the most!**

---

## So What Should You Do?

| If you… | Start with… |
|---------|-------------|
| Want cinematic / abstract / dream-like art | Try **Midjourney** first |
| Want ads / products / brand work / better character consistency | Start with **Nanobanana PRO** |
| Don't know yet | Start with **Nanobanana PRO** (most versatile) |

**You can succeed with either.**

This phase is not about collecting tools.
It's about **finding your workflow**.

Every lesson in this phase is designed to give you **options, not rules**.
